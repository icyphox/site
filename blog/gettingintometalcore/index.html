Getting Into Metalcore | 02 Dec, 2016 (2 min)

You’ve mentioned metalcore a couple of times, what is it?

Metalcore is a genre of extreme music, which blends hardcore punk and metal. This turns to sound much heavier than normal metal. Metalcore has a few interesting characteristics that sets it apart from mainstream metal.

First off, breakdowns. This is a huge difference between the two, mainly because it adds a bit more heaviness to the music. Breakdowns can, in some ways be compared to bass drops. The guitaring climbs and the vocals soar, and then there’s the typical pre-breakdown BLEGH and then there’s heavy chugging.

Some of the main artists in the genre are:

  • Killswitch Engage (I don’t listen to them)
  • August Burns Red (Christian metalcore)
  • Breakdown of Sanity
  • Miss May I (Melodic metalcore)

The list could go on, but that isn’t the point of this post.
Now onto how I got into this stuff.

How I got into Metalcore

So this happened some 4 years ago. I used to be a big time Linkin Park fan, yeah, everyone goes through that phase (there are some who refuse to get out of it though :P).
The only thing I used to listen to was Linkin Park. Obviously, I got bored, and decided to look for some other stuff like Linkin Park.
Back then, I didn’t know what genre I was listening to, I just called it rock. Though that isn’t entirely wrong, LP is more than that, they’re Nu-Metal.

Well anyway, I started by Googling “Bands like Linkin Park”. Yeah lol, I was pretty lame back then. And I found a question on Yahoo Answers (then again, what do you not find there?), which was what I was looking for.
There were a couple of answers, and one of them suggested Dangerkids. They aren’t very active now, but they sound very much like LP. But they had a lot more growls, and it was waaayyy heavier than LP.

Only then did I realize… this wasn’t nu-metal, but it was actually metalcore… (epic music plays).

So long story short, I Googled up some more bands, and eventually got into the *core.

Yeah that’s it. Go check out r/metalcore, you might find something of interest.
And for people coming from pop, or EDM, you can try some of the less heavier bands, listed below:

  • Erra
  • The Contortionist (more prog metal, right now)
  • Periphery
  • I See Stars (super electronic influenced)

Oka. Stick around.
